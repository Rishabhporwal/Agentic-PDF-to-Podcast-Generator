# Podcast Script

[Intro music plays]

Host: Welcome to "Wind of Change," a podcast discussing the latest developments and trends in the wind energy industry. I'm your host, [Name], and today we're exploring Vestas' corporate strategy, service offerings, and sustainability initiatives.

[Sound effect: A gentle breeze]

Host: Let's start with Vestas' corporate strategy. In their 2024 annual report, Vestas emphasizes its commitment to reducing carbon emissions, promoting social responsibility, and leading the transition towards a world powered by sustainable energy.

[Clip of Vestas CEO speaking at an event]

Vestas CEO: "We want to achieve carbon neutrality by 2030. We also aim to reduce our scope 1 and 2 GHG emissions by 55% by 2025 and 100% by 2030, as well as our scope 3 GHG emissions per MWh generated by 45% by 2030."

Host: Vestas' commitment to sustainability is not limited to reducing emissions. The company also aims to produce zero-waste wind turbines by 2040 and has implemented initiatives to improve material efficiency and increase the rate of refurbished component utilization.

[Sound effect: A recycling truck driving away]

Host: Now, let's shift our focus to Vestas' service offerings. In their 2024 annual report, Vestas highlights its scalability in a global service business, with 155 GW under service at the end of 2024 compared to 149 GW in 2023.

[Clip of Vestas Service employee speaking]

Vestas Service Employee: "Our active service contracts span more than 56,000 wind turbines across 71 countries. We're proud to be the largest Service business in the industry."

Host: Vestas is also committed to maximizing value creation with its customers. The company has streamlined its operations and integrated Utopus into the Service business, allowing for expanded digital offerings.

[Sound effect: A computer keyboard typing away]

Host: In addition to its service offerings, Vestas is driving sustainability in its own operations. The company aims to make all new service vehicles zero-emission from 2025 and has already achieved significant progress, with 25% of its total service fleet consisting of renewably fuelled vehicles.

[Clip of Vestas Hydrogen-Powered CTV speaking]

Vestas Hydrogen-Powered CTV: "We've tested the world's first hydrogen-powered crew transfer vessel (CTV) in 2022. This innovative technology will play a crucial role in reducing emissions in our offshore operations."

Host: That concludes today's episode of "Wind of Change." Join us next time as we explore more exciting developments and trends in the wind energy industry.

[Outro music plays]

Host: Thanks for tuning in, and don't forget to subscribe to our podcast for future episodes.
